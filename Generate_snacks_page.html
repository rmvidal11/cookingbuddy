Turon

Turon is a Filipino snack made with ripe plantains and jackfruit, wrapped in spring roll wrapper and fried until crispy. It is commonly sweetened with brown sugar.

Ingredients:

  • Ripe Plantains
  • Jackfruit
  • Spring Roll Wrapper
  • Brown Sugar
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice plantains lengthwise.
  2. Place a slice of jackfruit on a spring roll wrapper, add a slice of plantain, and sprinkle with brown sugar.
  3. Roll the wrapper, sealing the edges with water.
  4. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.
  5. Drain on paper towels and serve hot.

Halo-Halo

Halo-Halo is a popular Filipino dessert/snack that consists of a mixture of shaved ice and evaporated milk, sweetened beans, coconut, fruits, and various jellies. It is often topped with ice cream.

Ingredients:

  • Shaved Ice
  • Evaporated Milk
  • Sweetened Beans (Red Munggo and White Beans)
  • Coconut (Macapuno)
  • Assorted Fruits (Banana, Jackfruit, Sweet Potato, Ube)
  • Gelatin (Agar Agar)
  • Leche Flan
  • Ice Cream
  • Pinipig (Toasted Rice)

Instructions:

  1. Layer shaved ice in a glass.
  2. Add sweetened beans, coconut, fruits, and jellies.
  3. Drizzle with evaporated milk.
  4. Top with leche flan, ice cream, and pinipig.
  5. Mix everything together before eating.

Puto

Puto is a Filipino steamed rice cake that is often eaten as a snack or dessert. It is soft, fluffy, and slightly sweet. It is commonly served with grated coconut or butter.

Ingredients:

  • Rice Flour
  • Sugar
  • Baking Powder
  • Salt
  • Coconut Milk
  • Water
  • Grated Cheese (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Mix rice flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add coconut milk and water, and mix until smooth.
  3. Pour into molds and steam for about 20 minutes.
  4. Remove from molds and cool before serving.
